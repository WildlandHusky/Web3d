Soft drinks


On May 8, 1886, Coca-Cola was born in Atlanta, Georgia, USA. Since then, it has intermingled with social development, inspired innovation, and co-sponsored the famous Emory University.

After entering the 21st century, 1.7 billion people around the world drink Coca-Cola's products every day, and about 19,400 bottles of drinks are sold every second. In October 2016, Coca-Cola ranked the third place in the world's 100 most valuable brands in 2016 [1]. Coca-Cola provides Chinese consumers with more than 50 beverage choices from more than 15 brands and 150 million cups of its products are enjoyed in China every day. Since its return to the Chinese market in 1979, Coca-Cola has invested more than 9 billion US dollars by the end of 2014. By the end of 2020, it has built 43 factories in China with 45,000 employees. Coca-Cola and its bottlers have a long history in China of supporting education and public welfare, promoting environmental protection and helping local communities develop. Coca-Cola is also a full sponsor of the Special Olympics, Olympic Games, Paralympic Games, World Expo, Universiade and Youth Olympic Games held in China.
